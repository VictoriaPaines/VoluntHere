Dunkirk Refugee Women's Centre


Who are they?

“Our aim and purpose is to provide close support to female refugees. To enable us to do this, we are present on the ground every day in the woodland and open grassland where the women and their families are living and trying to find shelter. We work in an all-female team of 3 to 7 volunteers. Together we try to fulfil their basic material needs on an individual basis, and spend time with them organising activities such as English classes. For more specialised need (medical, legal or psychological), we help by connecting them to professional organisations. We recognise that “basic needs” include mental health wellbeing and, therefore, we do what we can to offer moral support. We have created spaces that are dedicated to helping the women feel comfortable, whether their wish is to talk to someone or to be left alone. Using a van, blankets, and tarp to create temporary safe spaces, the team on the ground continues to provide close support to female refugees in Dunkirk, and has more recently started to do so in Calais as well.”


What would volunteering with them involve?

“As part of the team, volunteers need to respond to variable and ever-changing situations. It is necessary to be adaptable and patient.”

  • Distributing goods.
  • Assisting those in need of hospital access.
  • Connecting people with legal support.
  • Connecting people with legal support.
  • And more.”


How are they different to other charities?

Exclusively female in volunteers and those helped.


Accommodation offered?

Yes – accommodation in Calais. 5 euros per night and free after a month of volunteering.


Particular skills they look for?

  • Must be female or non-binary and over 18.
  • Knowledge of and ability to translate French, Kurdish (Sorani), Pashto, Farsi, Arabic.
  • Experience in working with vulnerable people.
  • Experience and desire to run activities and workshops of any kind.
  • Some form of medical training is a distinct advantage.

Testimonials:

“arriving at the camp last November I didn’t expect to meet or work with so many resilient and strong women from all over the world.”
